Complete Guide to SLA Calculator Features

🎯 Understanding SLA Calculations

Service Level Agreements (SLAs) define the expected uptime for your service. This calculator helps you understand how much downtime is allowed and what it means for your operations.

📊
Industry Standard

All SLA calculations are based on calendar time (24/7) as per industry standards

⚡
Common SLA Levels

99.9% = 8h 45m/year • 99.95% = 4h 23m/year • 99.99% = 52m/year

🔧 Work Start & End Times Explained

⚠️
Key Point: Work times don't change your allowed downtime. They help you understand the business impact if all downtime happens during work hours.
📊
Your SLA calculation stays the same

Allowed downtime is always calculated using calendar time (industry standard). Work times don't affect this.

🎯
Shows "Work Impact" analysis

When enabled, shows worst-case availability if all your allowed downtime occurred during work hours.

💡 Real-World Examples

🏢 Standard Office (9 AM - 5 PM)

Setup: Work Start: 9:00 AM, Work End: 5:00 PM

Result: See how 99.9% SLA (43 min downtime/month) impacts an 8-hour workday

Use case: Office applications, internal tools, customer support systems

🌙 Night Shift Operations (11 PM - 7 AM)

Setup: Work Start: 11:00 PM, Work End: 7:00 AM

Result: Calculate impact for overnight operations and manufacturing

Use case: Manufacturing, security systems, overnight call centers

⚡ 24/7 Critical Systems

Setup: Don't enable "Analyze Work Time Impact"

Result: Focus on pure SLA metrics without work hour analysis

Use case: Healthcare systems, emergency services, global web services

ℹ️
How it works: The calculator takes your allowed downtime (based on calendar time) and shows what your effective availability would be if that downtime occurred during your specified work hours.

⚙️ Advanced Features Guide

📅 Include Weekends

Purpose: Controls whether weekends count in your SLA calculation.

✅
Enabled (Default)

24/7 service - weekends included in calculations

❌
Disabled

Business days only (Mon-Fri) - reduces allowed downtime

💡 When to use: Disable for services that only operate during weekdays (internal tools, office applications).
⏰ Analyze Work Time Impact

Purpose: Shows worst-case business impact if all downtime happens during work hours.

📊 SLA Calculation Always based on calendar time (industry standard)
🎯 Work Impact Shows availability if downtime hits work hours
💡 When to use: Understanding business impact during active work periods, planning maintenance windows.
⚡ Work Hours per Week

Purpose: Set total weekly hours for accurate work impact calculations.

40 hours: Full-time standard office 50 hours: Extended office hours 20 hours: Part-time operations 168 hours: 24/7 operations

📊 Calculation Methods

Standard Mode (Default)
Allowed Downtime = (100% - SLA%) × Calendar Time
24/7 calculation including all hours and weekends
Weekdays Only Mode
Allowed Downtime = (100% - SLA%) × (Calendar Time × 5/7)
Business days only (Monday through Friday)
Work Impact Analysis
Base SLA = Calendar Time Calculation
Work Impact = (Work Time - Calendar Downtime) / Work Time
Shows worst-case scenario where all downtime affects work hours

📈 Practical Example

For 99.9% SLA over 1 month:

Standard: 43.2 minutes downtime allowed
Work Impact (40h/week): Same 43.2 minutes downtime, but shows impact if it all happens during work hours
Weekend Excluded: 30.9 minutes downtime (weekdays only)
